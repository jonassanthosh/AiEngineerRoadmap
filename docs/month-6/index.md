# Month 6: Building New Models

The final month shifts from using existing models to creating new ones. You'll learn to read research papers, understand architectural innovations, train models at scale, evaluate them rigorously, and think about safety and alignment — then put it all together in a final capstone.

## What You'll Cover

| Lesson | Topic |
|--------|-------|
| 1 | [Reading Papers](reading-papers) — how to read, evaluate, and implement ML research |
| 2 | [Architecture Innovations](architecture-innovations) — GQA, MoE, state-space models, and what's next |
| 3 | [Pretraining at Scale](pretraining-at-scale) — data curation, training recipes, and scaling strategies |
| 4 | [Evaluation](evaluation) — benchmarks, human evaluation, and building eval pipelines |
| 5 | [Safety & Alignment](safety-alignment) — red-teaming, RLHF pitfalls, responsible deployment |
| 6 | [Career & Portfolio](career-portfolio) — AI engineering roles, building a portfolio, interview prep |
| 7 | [**Capstone: Design a Novel Architecture**](month6-project) — identify a weakness, propose a fix, implement and evaluate it |

## Prerequisites

Months 1–5. This month assumes strong familiarity with the full LLM stack — architecture, training, optimization, and deployment.

## Capstone Project

Your final project follows a real research workflow: identify a weakness in an existing architecture, propose a modification, implement it, and rigorously evaluate whether it helps. This is how architectural innovations like GQA, RMSNorm, and SwiGLU were developed.

## Estimated Time

**~60–80 hours** total (15–20 hours/week over 4 weeks).
