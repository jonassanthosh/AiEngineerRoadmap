# Month 4: Large Language Models

Now that you understand the Transformer, it's time to see what happens when you scale it up. This month covers the architectures, training methods, and infrastructure behind large language models — GPT, BERT, and their descendants.

## What You'll Cover

| Lesson | Topic |
|--------|-------|
| 1 | [Scaling Laws](scaling-laws) — how model size, data, and compute interact |
| 2 | [GPT Architecture](gpt-architecture) — decoder-only Transformers and autoregressive generation |
| 3 | [BERT and Beyond](bert-and-beyond) — encoder models, masked language modeling, and variants |
| 4 | [Tokenizers](tokenizers) — BPE, WordPiece, SentencePiece, and their trade-offs |
| 5 | [Training Infrastructure](training-infrastructure) — distributed training, data parallelism, mixed precision |
| 6 | [nanoGPT](nanogpt) — building and training a GPT model from scratch |
| 7 | [**Project: Train a Language Model**](month4-project) — your own GPT on a custom dataset |

## Prerequisites

Months 1–3, especially the Transformer architecture and attention mechanism from Month 3.

## Capstone Project

You'll build a complete language model training pipeline — data collection, tokenization, model definition, training, evaluation, and text generation — training a small GPT (5–50M parameters) on a dataset of your choice.

## Estimated Time

**~60–80 hours** total (15–20 hours/week over 4 weeks).
